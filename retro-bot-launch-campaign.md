## 3. Key Messages

**Core message (the idea that travels):**
Most teams optimize the prompt. The best teams optimize the process.

**Supporting messages:**

1. *The problem:* AI sessions don't improve by default. Every session starts where the
   last one ended — at zero. The teams pulling ahead are the ones who've built the loop.

2. *The solution:* The retrospective is the oldest improvement mechanism in software
   engineering. Retro-bot brings it to AI collaboration: a structured reflection after
   each session that produces versioned changes to your setup.

3. *The proof:* We built this for our own work at Gather. Every retro we run produces
   a dated snapshot and an audit trail. After 10 retros, you can see exactly how your
   Claude setup has evolved — and why.

4. *The philosophy:* Shared understanding turns into collective progress. Open-sourcing
   this isn't charity — it's how we think. If you work with AI seriously, this is for you.

**Differentiation:**
Gather doesn't just deploy AI for clients. We have a methodology for making AI
collaboration get systematically better over time. Retro-bot is that methodology,
made tangible.

**Tone:** Confident and generous. Not "look what I built" — "here's something I think
matters and I want you to have it."

## 7. LinkedIn Launch Post — Draft Framework

**Hook (don't lead with the tool):**
> "Every Claude session I've run ends the same way. I close the window. The lessons disappear.
> I open a new one and start from scratch.
> That's not a prompt problem. It's a systems problem."

**Body (the insight):**
> Agile teams solved this 30 years ago with the sprint retrospective. You don't just ship —
> you reflect. You identify what worked, what didn't, and what you change next time.
> The best teams don't just get better at the work. They get better at getting better.
>
> I've been thinking about why AI workflows don't compound the same way. And I think it's
> because most people optimize the session. Nobody optimizes the system.
>
> So I built retro-bot: an open-source skill for Claude that runs an agile retrospective
> after any collaboration session. It surfaces what worked, what didn't, applies versioned
> changes to your setup, and saves a dated snapshot to a running audit trail.
>
> After 10 retros, you can look back and see exactly how your Claude workflow has evolved.
> The prompts are better. The skills are tighter. The sessions are faster.
> That's compounding.

**Close (generous, not promotional):**
> Sharing it openly because I believe shared understanding turns into collective progress.
> It's how we think at Gather — and I'd rather prove it than say it.
>
> GitHub → [link]
> Built with @Gather | Contributions welcome
